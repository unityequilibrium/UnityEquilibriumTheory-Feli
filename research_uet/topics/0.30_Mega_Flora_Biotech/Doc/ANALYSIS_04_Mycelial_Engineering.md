## 1. The Fungal "Internet" Concept
In a natural forest, the "Wood Wide Web" (Mycorrhiza) connects trees. In a **UET Urban Ecosystem**, we engineer this network to serve as a high-speed nutrient logistics system.

## 2. Fungal Engineering (Genetic Modification)
We don't just use wild fungi; we engineer them for specific urban tasks:
*   **Hyper-Decomposition:** Genes modified to produce advanced enzymes (Cellulases, Lignin Peroxidases) that break down urban organic waste (food, paper) in hours instead of months.
*   **Mineralization Path:** Converting complex organic molecules directly into ionic N-P-K (Nitrogen, Phosphorus, Potassium) that is immediately bio-available.
*   **Bi-Directional Signaling:** Fungi act as a sensor network. When a tree is under "Structural Stress" (Wind, Weight), it sends a chemical signal (Jasmonic Acid) which the fungi detect.

## 3. The Urban Bio-Loop (Automation)
The "Cheat Mode" isn't a hack; it's a closed-loop economy:
1.  **Input:** Human/Household Waste (The "Organic Battery").
2.  **Processing:** **Bio-Digesters** (Fungal Hubs) mineralize the waste.
3.  **Distribution:** The Mycelial Network shunts the nutrient flux to the trees that need it most (Smart Allocation).
4.  **Efficiency:** This turns "Waste Management" from a cost into "Infrastructure Growth."

## 4. Why it isn't "Cheat"
Nature does this, but slowly. We just optimize the **Flux Rate**. By increasing the nutrient transfer speed by 10x, we enable "Year 1 Readiness" for mega-flora bridges and buildings.
